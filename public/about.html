Timeline.wang is an interactive timeline created by Ainsley Rutterford and James O'Reilly as part of the third year computer science unit "Web Technologies" at the University of Bristol. The name for the website was inspired by our flatmate and close friend Justin Wang (in retrospect, this domain name was perhaps not the most sensible choice).

While websites such as Google and Wikipedia provide essential utilities to the public, the websites which are most memorable to us are those which present unique and captivating experiences. These websites often cause us to view something with a fresh perspective. We want each user that visits this website to be filled with a sense of intrigue and curiosity as they experience modern human history in an immersive and interactive way.

We believe in the importance of history both as a reminder of both our past failures and past successes, providing direction and hope as we are faced with the challenges of the 21st century. Constructing timelines allow us to see how humanity has progressed, not only in general, but within specific fields such as medicine, space exploration and computer science.

Progress in Medicine: From the disovery of penicillin by Alexander Fleming in 1928 to the eradication of smallpox in 1977 and the invention gene editing technologies like CRISPR recently. Progress in Space Exploration: From Laika, the canine comsonaut in 1957 to the Apollo 11 Moon landing in 1969 and more recently, SpaceX and the invention of reusable rockets. Progress in Computer Science: From the development of the C programming language in the 1970's, to its implementation in IBM's Deep Blue chess artificial intelligence, which famously defeated Gary Kasparov in 1997.

When designing this website, we decided to avoid using frameworks wherever possible. We viewed this as an opportunity to learn and preferred to not abstract away anything that we did not already fully understand. It is rewarding to know that every element of this website was designed and implemented by us alone. We may use frameworks going forward now that we have a full understanding of the details that frameworks abstract.

In the "Profile" page, each user is assigned an avatar on page refresh. We wanted to create avatars that fit with the philosophy of the website. We designed old geometry constructions, edited to look as they were written with ink on an academic paper. To us, these avatars symbolise curiosity and the boundless pursuit of knowledge.
